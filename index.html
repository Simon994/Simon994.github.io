My Education

I got my DPhil (PhD) from the University of Oxford, in Physical and Theoretical Chemistry, in 2012.

My DPhil (Oxford's fancy name for a PhD) spanned a lot of areas: physics, chemistry, biochemistry, to name just a few. At its core though, was the development and building of very sensitive instruments to detect tiny changes in the absorption of substances. As part of this, I used LabVIEW (a graphical programming language) to control the instruments' data collection and analysis. I also did data analysis using MATLAB. Having constructed the intruments, I used them to investigate fundamental precesses behind bird navigation.

Before my DPhil, I received my Masters in Chemistry from the University of Leicester, where I graduated top of my class. I was awarded four prizes for examination performance, in consecutive years, including the GlaxoSmithKline Prize and Hunter Medal for best graduating MChem student. I also recieved an Undergraduate Nuffield Research Bursary, which I used for a project in computational chemistry.

I wrote and published lots of articles during my time in Oxford and beyond. Being an author is what made me want to be an editor! You can take a look at some of my articles in the later section, on Science!

Editor

After completing my DPhil, I pursued a career in scientific publishing, first at Springer Nature, then at the Royal Society of Chemistry. I spent seven years in this industry, continually progressing to ultimatly become the head of a portfolio of well-respected scientific journals.

As Executive Editor, I set the journals' strategic direction and had sigificant budget and team management responsibilities. Amoung many activites, I travelled to top universities around the world, from MIT to Tsinghua, to meet and collaborate with leading scientists. I also had great opportunities to work with technology, UX and data science teams to improve our products and customer service.
